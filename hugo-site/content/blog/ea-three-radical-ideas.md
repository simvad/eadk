title: "EA is three radical ideas I want to protect"
date: 2023-11-13
category: "Meta"
---

Effective altruism contains three radical ideas that I don't easily find in other communities. These three ideas are ideas I want to protect.

**Radical Empathy.** Humanity has long had a fairly narrow moral circle. Radical Empathy is the idea that there are many groups of people, or other entities, that are worthy of our moral consideration even if we don't naturally feel empathy towards them. This includes people in distant countries, future generations, and non-human animals.

**Scope Sensitivity.** When we care about doing good, the scale of the impact matters enormously. Saving 1,000 lives is roughly 1,000 times better than saving one life, and we should make decisions accordingly. This might seem obvious, but psychologically humans are terrible at scope sensitivity.

**Scout Mindset.** Rather than defending our existing beliefs like a soldier, we should seek to understand reality as clearly as possible, like a scout mapping terrain. This means actively looking for evidence that might change our minds and being willing to update our views.

These three ideas together form a powerful framework for thinking about how to do good in the world. They push us to care more broadly, think more carefully about scale, and be more honest with ourselves about what works.
